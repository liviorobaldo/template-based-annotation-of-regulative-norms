IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR any person
TO give evidence on any matter, and to answer any question put to him in the course of giving evidence
WHEN/IF/WHERE in proceedings in which a court is hearing an application for an order under Part IV or V,
EVEN IF doing so might incriminate him or his spouse or civil partner of an offence.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a statement or admission made in such proceedings
TO be admissible in evidence against the person making it or his spouse or civil partner in proceedings for an offence other than perjury.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a court
TO exercise the High Court’s inherent jurisdiction with respect to children, (a)so as to require a child to be placed in the care, or put under the supervision, of a local authority, (b)so as to require a child to be accommodated by or on behalf of a local authority, (c)so as to make a child who is the subject of a care order a ward of court, or (d)for the purpose of conferring on any local authority power to determine any question which has arisen, or which may arise, in connection with any aspect of parental responsibility for a child.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR any application for any exercise of the court’s inherent jurisdiction with respect to children
TO be made by a local authority 
UNLESS the authority have obtained the leave of the court.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the court
TO grant leave
ONLY IF it is satisfied that, (a)the result which the authority wish to achieve could not be achieved through the making of any order of a kind to which subsection (5) applies, and (b)there is reasonable cause to believe that if the court’s inherent jurisdiction is not exercised with respect to the child he is likely to suffer significant harm.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the Secretary of State
TO make regulations providing (a)for prescribed orders which (i)are made by a court in Northern Ireland, and (ii)appear to the Secretary of State to correspond in their effect to orders which may be made under any provision of this Act, to have effect in prescribed circumstances, for prescribed purposes of this Act, as if they were orders of a prescribed kind made under this Act, (b)for prescribed orders which (i)are made by a court in England and Wales, and (ii)appear to the Secretary of State to correspond in their effect to orders which may be made under any provision in force in Northern Ireland, to have effect in prescribed circumstances, for prescribed purposes of the law of Northern Ireland, as if they were orders of a prescribed kind made in Northern Ireland.

FOR the Secretary of State
TO make regulations providing for prescribed orders which, (a)are made by a court in the Isle of Man or in any of the Channel Islands, and (b)appear to the Secretary of State to correspond in their effect to orders which may be made under this Act, to have effect in prescribed circumstances for prescribed purposes of this Act, as if they were orders of a prescribed kind made under this Act.

TO (a)make such consequential amendments (including repeals) in, (i)section 25 of the Children and Young Persons Act 1969 (transfers between England and Wales and Northern Ireland) or (ii)section 26 (transfers between England and Wales and Channel Islands or Isle of Man) of that Act, as the Secretary of State considers necessary or expedient, and (b)modify any provision of this Act, in its application (by virtue of the regulations) in relation to an order made otherwise than in England and Wales.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the court
TO issue a warrant authorising any constable to assist that person in the exercise of those powers, using reasonable force if necessary
WHEN/IF/WHERE on an application made by any person for a warrant under this section, it appears to the court, (a)that a person attempting to exercise powers under any enactment mentioned in subsection (6) has been prevented from doing so by being refused entry to the premises concerned or refused access to the child concerned or (b)that any such person is likely to be so prevented from exercising any such powers.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR every warrant issued under this section
TO be addressed to, and executed by, a constable who shall be accompanied by the person applying for the warrant
WHEN/IF/WHERE (a)that person so desires, and (b)the court by whom the warrant is issued does not direct otherwise.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court granting an application for a warrant under this section
TO direct that the constable concerned may, in executing the warrant, be accompanied by a registered medical practitioner, registered nurse or registered midwife if he so chooses.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR an application for a warrant under this section
TO be made in the manner and form prescribed by the rules of court.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the application and any warrant granted on the application
TO name the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE (a)an application for a warrant under this section relates to a particular child, and (b)it is reasonably practicable to do so,
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the application and any warrant granted on the application
TO describe the child as clearly as possible
WHEN/IF/WHERE it does not name him
WHEN/IF/WHERE (a)an application for a warrant under this section relates to a particular child, and (b)it is reasonably practicable to do so,
