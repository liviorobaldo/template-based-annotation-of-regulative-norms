------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a court 
TO make a special guardianship order with respect to a child 
WHEN/IF/WHERE deciding about any family proceedings in which a question arises with respect to the welfare of the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE —
(a) an application for the order has been made by an individual who falls within subsection (3)(a) or (b) (or more than one such individual jointly); or
(b) the court considers that a special guardianship order should be made even though no such application has been made
SUBJECT TO section 29(5) and (6) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED 
FOR an individual 
TO make an application under subsection (3) or (6)(a) 
UNLESS, before the beginning of the period of three months ending with the date of the application, he has given written notice of his intention to make the application—
(a) if the child in question is being looked after by a local authority, to that local authority, or
(b) otherwise, to the local authority in whose area the individual is ordinarily resident
SUBJECT TO section 29(5) and (6) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a local authority
TO investigate the matter and prepare a report for the court dealing with—
(a) the suitability of the applicant to be a special guardian;
(b) such matters (if any) as may be prescribed by the Secretary of State; and
(c) any other matter which the local authority consider to be relevant.
WHEN/IF/WHERE receiving such a notice
SUBJECT TO section 29(5) and (6) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a court
TO ask a local authority to conduct such an investigation and prepare such a report
SUBJECT TO section 29(5) and (6) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a local authority 
TO do so
WHEN/IF/WHERE the court requests so
SUBJECT TO section 29(5) and (6) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a local authority
TO make such arrangements as they see fit for any person to act on their behalf in connection with conducting an investigation or preparing a report referred to in subsection (8) or (9)
SUBJECT TO section 29(5) and (6) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED 
FOR a court 
TO make a special guardianship order 
UNLESS it has received a report dealing with the matters referred to in subsection (8).
SUBJECT TO section 29(5) and (6) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a court
BEFORE making a special guardianship order
TO consider whether, if the order were made—
(a) a child arrangements order containing contact provision should also be made with respect to the child, . . .
(b) any section 8 order in force with respect to the child should be varied or discharged.
(c) where a provision contained in a child arrangements order made with respect to the child is not discharged, any enforcement order relating to that provision should be revoked, and
(d) where an activity direction has been made—
(i) in proceedings for the making, variation or discharge of a child arrangements order with respect to the child, or
(ii) in other proceedings that relate to such an order,
that direction should be discharged.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a court
TO —
(a) give leave for the child to be known by a new surname;
(b) grant the leave required by section 14C(3)(b), either generally or for specified purposes.
WHEN/IF/WHERE making a special guardianship order.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a special guardian appointed by an order
TO parental responsibility for the child in respect of whom it is made.
WHEN/IF/WHERE the order is in force
SUBJECT TO section 29(7) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a special guardian
TO exercise parental responsibility to the exclusion of any other person with parental responsibility for the child (apart from another special guardian)
SUBJECT TO any other order in force with respect to the child under this Act.
SUBJECT TO section 29(7) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED 
FOR a person
TO (a) cause the child to be known by a new surname; or
(b) remove him from the United Kingdom,
without either the written consent of every person who has parental responsibility for the child or the leave of the court.
WHEN/IF/WHERE a special guardianship order is in force with respect to a child
UNLESS the removal of the child, for a period of less than three months, is done by a special guardian of his.
SUBJECT TO section 29(7) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a special guardian 
TO take reasonable steps to give notice of that fact to—
(a) each parent of a child with parental responsibility; and
(b) each guardian of the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child with respect to whom a special guardianship order is in force dies
SUBJECT TO section 29(7) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR any other special guardian
TO not give notice as respects that parent or guardian
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child with respect to whom a special guardianship order is in force dies
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child has more than one special guardian
SUBJECT TO section 29(7) of the Adoption and Children Act 2002.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a court
TO vary or discharge a special guardianship order
WHEN/IF/WHERE receiving an the application of—
(a) the special guardian (or any of them, if there are more than one);
(b) any parent or guardian of the child concerned;
(c) any individual who is named in a child arrangements order as a person with whom the child is to live;
(d) any individual not falling within any of paragraphs (a) to (c) who has, or immediately before the making of the special guardianship order had, parental responsibility for the child;
(e) the child himself; or
(f)a local authority designated in a care order with respect to the child.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a court
TO vary or discharge the special guardianship order 
WHEN/IF/WHERE it considers that the order should be varied or discharged, even though no application has been made under subsection (1)
WHEN/IF/WHERE it is deciding about any family proceedings in which a question arises with respect to the welfare of a child with respect to whom a special guardianship order is in force.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR (a) the child;
(b) any parent or guardian of his;
(c) any step-parent of his who has acquired, and has not lost, parental responsibility for him by virtue of section 4A;
(d) any individual falling within subsection (1)(d) who immediately before the making of the special guardianship order had, but no longer has, parental responsibility for him.
TO obtain the leave of the court 
BEFORE making an application under subsection (1).
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a court
TO grant leave
WHEN/IF/WHERE the person applying for leave to make an application under subsection (1) is the child
ONLY IF it is satisfied that he has sufficient understanding to make the proposed application under subsection (1).
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a court
TO not grant leave to a person falling within subsection (3)(b)(c) or (d) 
UNLESS it is satisfied that there has been a significant change in circumstances since the making of the special guardianship order.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a court
TO (in the light of any provision in rules of court that is of the kind mentioned in section 11(2)(a) or (b))—
(a) draw up a timetable with a view to determining the question without delay; and
(b) give such directions as it considers appropriate for the purpose of ensuring, so far as is reasonably practicable, that the timetable is adhered to
WHEN/IF/WHERE deciding about proceedings in which any question of making, varying or discharging a special guardianship order arises.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR A special guardianship order, or an order varying one
TO contain provisions which are to have effect for a specified period.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR a local authority 
TO make arrangements for the provision within their area of special guardianship support services, which means—
(a) counselling, advice and information; and
(b) such other services as are prescribed,
in relation to special guardianship.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR a local authority
TO make regulations under subsection (1)(b) so as to secure that local authorities provide financial support.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a local authority 
TO carry out an assessment of a person’s needs for special guardianship support services
WHEN/IF/WHERE receiving the request of any of the following persons—
(a) a child with respect to whom a special guardianship order is in force;
(b) a special guardian;
(c) a parent;
(d) any other person who falls within a prescribed description,
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a local authority 
TO carry out an assessment of a person’s needs for special guardianship support services
WHEN/IF/WHERE he is a person of a prescribed description, or his case falls within a prescribed description, or both he and his case fall within prescribed descriptions)
WHEN/IF/WHERE the Secretary of State so provides in regulations
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a local authority 
TO carry out an assessment of that person’s needs for special guardianship support services
WHEN/IF/WHERE receiving the request of any other person.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a local authority 
TO decide whether to provide special guardianship support services to a person
WHEN/IF/WHERE, as a result of an assessment, they decide that the person has need for such services.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a local authority 
TO prepare a plan in accordance with which special guardianship support services are to be provided to him, and keep the plan under review
WHEN/IF/WHERE —
(a) a local authority decide to provide any special guardianship support services to a person, and
(b) the circumstances fall within a prescribed description.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR The Secretary of State
TO, by regulations, make provision about assessments, preparing and reviewing plans, the provision of special guardianship support services in accordance with plans and reviewing the provision of special guardianship support services.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR The regulations 
TO make provision—
(a) about the type of assessment which is to be carried out, or the way in which an assessment is to be carried out;
(b) about the way in which a plan is to be prepared;
(c) about the way in which, and the time at which, a plan or the provision of special guardianship support services is to be reviewed;
(d) about the considerations to which a local authority are to have regard in carrying out an assessment or review or preparing a plan;
(e) as to the circumstances in which a local authority may provide special guardianship support services subject to conditions (including conditions as to payment for the support or the repayment of financial support);
(f) as to the consequences of conditions imposed by virtue of paragraph (e) not being met (including the recovery of any financial support provided);
(g) as to the circumstances in which this section may apply to a local authority in respect of persons who are outside that local authority’s area;
(h) as to the circumstances in which a local authority may recover from another local authority the expenses of providing special guardianship support services to any person.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR A local authority
TO provide special guardianship support services (or any part of them) by securing their provision by—
(a) another local authority; or
(b) a person within a description prescribed in regulations of persons who may provide special guardianship support services,
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR A local authority
TO also arrange with any such authority or person for that other authority or that person to carry out the local authority’s functions in relation to assessments under this section.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR A local authority
TO carry out an assessment of the needs of any person for the purposes of this section at the same time as an assessment of his needs is made under any other provision of this Act or under any other enactment.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a local authority 
TO establish a procedure for considering representations (including complaints) made to them by any person to whom they may provide special guardianship support services about the discharge of their functions under section 14F in relation to him.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR Regulations
TO be made by the Secretary of State imposing time limits on the making of representations under subsection (1).
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a local authority 
TO comply with regulations (if any) made by the Secretary of State for the purposes of this subsection
WHEN/IF/WHERE considering representations under subsection (1).
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a court
TO make an order requiring—
(a) an officer of the Serviceor a Welsh family proceedings officer to be made available; or
(b) a local authority to make an officer of the authority available,
to advise, assist and (where appropriate) befriend any person named in the order
WHEN/IF/WHERE, in any family proceedings, the court has power to make an order under this Part with respect to any child.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED 
FOR a court 
TO make a family assistance order 
UNLESS it has obtained the consent of every person to be named in the order other than the child.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a family assistance order 
TO direct—
(a) the person named in the order; or
(b) such of the persons named in the order as may be specified in the order,
to take such steps as may be so specified with a view to enabling the officer concerned to be kept informed of the address of any person named in the order and to be allowed to visit any such person.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED 
FOR a family assistance order 
TO direct an officer concerned to give advice and assistance as regards establishing, improving and maintaining contact to such of the persons named in the order as may be specified in the order
WHEN/IF/WHERE the court makes a family assistance order with respect to a child and the order is to be in force at the same time as a contact provision contained in a child arrangements order made with respect to the child.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a family assistance order 
TO have effect for a period of twelve months beginning with the day on which it is made.
UNLESS it specifies a shorter period.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR a family assistance order 
TO direct the officer concerned to report to the court on such matters relating to the section 8 order as the court may require (including the question whether the section 8 order ought to be varied or discharged)
WHEN/IF/WHERE the court makes a family assistance order with respect to a child and the order is to be in force at the same time as a section 8 order made with respect to the child, the family assistance order.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a family assistance order 
TO be made so as to require a local authority to make an officer of theirs available
UNLESS —
(a) the authority agree; or
(b) the child concerned lives or will live within their area.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY 
FOR an officer of the Service or a Welsh family proceedings officer
TO —
(a) make a risk assessment in relation to the child, and
(b) provide the risk assessment to the court.
WHEN/IF/WHERE, in carrying out any function to which this section applies, the officer is given cause to suspect that the child concerned is at risk of harm.
