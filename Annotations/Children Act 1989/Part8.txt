IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the person carrying on a private children’s home
TO (a)safeguard and promote the child’s welfare (b)make use of the services and facilities available for children cared for by their own parents as appears to that person reasonable in the case of the child (c)advise, assist and befriend him with a view to promoting his welfare when he ceases to be so accommodated
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child is accommodated in the home.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the person carrying on the home
TO, so far as is reasonably practicable, ascertain the wishes and feelings of—
(a) the child;
(b) his parents;
(c) any other person who is not a parent of his but who has parental responsibility for him; and
(d) any person whose wishes and feelings the person carrying on the home considers to be relevant,
regarding the matter to be decided
BEFORE making any decision with respect to any such child the person carrying on the home.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the person concerned
TO give due consideration (a)having regard to the child’s age and understanding, to such wishes and feelings of his as he has ascertained (b)to such other wishes and feelings mentioned in subsection (2) as he has been able to ascertain and
(c)to the child’s religious persuasion, racial origin and cultural and linguistic background
WHEN/IF/WHERE making any such decision
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a person (“P”) who is disqualified (under section 68) from fostering a child privately
TO carry on, or be otherwise concerned in the management of, or have any financial interest in, a children's home in England
UNLESS (a)P has, within the period of 28 days beginning with the day on which P became aware of P's disqualification, disclosed to the appropriate authority the fact that P is so disqualified, and
(b)P has obtained the appropriate authority's written consent.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a person (“E”)
TO employ a person (“P”) who is so disqualified in a children's home in England
UNLESS a)E has, within the period of 28 days beginning with the day on which E became aware of P's disqualification, disclosed to the appropriate authority the fact that P is so disqualified, and
(b)E has obtained the appropriate authority's written consent.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a person who is disqualified (under section 68) from fostering a child privately
TO carry on, or be otherwise concerned in the management of, or have any financial interest in a children’s home in Wales
UNLESS (a)disclosed to the appropriate authority the fact that he is so disqualified; and
(b)obtained its written consent.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a person
TO employ a person who is so disqualified in a children’s home in Wales
UNLESS he has (a)disclosed to the appropriate authority the fact that that person is so disqualified and (b)obtained its written consent.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR an appropriate authority
TO inform the applicant by a written notice which stating (a)the reason for the refusal (b)the applicant’s right to appeal under section 65A against the refusal to the First-tier Tribunal (c)the time within which he may do so
WHEN/IF/WHERE the appropriate authority refuses to give its consent under this section.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a Tribunal
TO confirm the authority’s decision or direct it to give the consent in question
WHEN/IF/WHERE receiving an appeal.
