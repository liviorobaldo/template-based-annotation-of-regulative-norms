FOR an order under subsection (4), an application for a warrant under this section and any such warrant
TO describe the child as clearly as possible
WHEN/IF/WHERE it does not name him.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a person
TO, knowingly and without lawful authority or reasonable excuse, 
(a) takes a child to whom this section applies away from the responsible person;
(b) keeps such a child away from the responsible person; or
(c) induces, assists or incites such a child to run away or stay away from the responsible person.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO make an order under this section (“a recovery order”)
WHEN/IF/WHERE it appears to the court that there is reason to believe that a child to whom this section applies—
(a) has been unlawfully taken away or is being unlawfully kept away from the responsible person;
(b) has run away or is staying away from the responsible person; or
(c) is missing,
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO make a recovery order 
ONLY IF it receives the application of—
(a) any person who has parental responsibility for the child by virtue of a care order or emergency protection order; or
(b) where the child is in police protection, the designated officer.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR A recovery order
TO name the child and—
(a) any person who has parental responsibility for the child by virtue of a care order or emergency protection order; or
(b) where the child is in police protection, the designated officer.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR Premises
TO be specified under subsection (3)(d)
ONLY IF it appears to the court that there are reasonable grounds for believing the child to be on them.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR an authorisation
TO identify the recovery order
WHEN/IF/WHERE a person is authorised as mentioned in subsection (7)(c).
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR any person claiming to be authorised
TO produce some duly authenticated document showing that he is so authorised
WHEN/IF/WHERE a person is authorised as mentioned in subsection (7)(c)
WHEN/IF/WHERE asked to do so
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a person
TO intentionally obstruct an authorised person exercising the power under subsection (3)(b) to remove a child.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR a person
TO comply with any request made under subsection (3)(c) on the ground that complying with it might incriminate him or his spouse or civil partner of an offence
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the Secretary of State
TO issue a certificate under this section with respect to that home
WHEN/IF/WHERE it is proposed to use a voluntary home or private children’s home to provide a refuge for children who appear to be at risk of harm.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the Secretary of State
TO issue a certificate under this section with respect to that foster parent
WHEN/IF/WHERE a local authority or voluntary organisation arrange for a foster parent to provide such a refuge.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the Secretary of State
TO, by regulations—
(a) make provision as to the manner in which certificates may be issued;
(b) impose requirements which must be complied with while any certificate is in force; and
(c) provide for the withdrawal of certificates in prescribed circumstances.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR rules of court
TO, Without prejudice to section 93 or any other power to make such rules, be made with respect to the procedure to be followed in connection with proceedings under this Part.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the rules
TO, in particular make provision—
(a) as to the form in which any application is to be made or direction is to be given;
(b) prescribing the persons who are to be notified of—
(i) the making, or extension, of an emergency protection order; or
(ii) the making of an application under section 45(4) or (8) or 46(7); and
(c) as to the content of any such notification and the manner in which, and person by whom, it is to be given.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The Secretary of State 
TO, by regulations provide that the authority shall be treated, for the purposes of this Act, as though they and not the original applicant had applied for, and been granted, the order
WHEN/IF/WHERE —
(a) an emergency protection order has been made with respect to a child;
(b) the applicant for the order was not the local authority within whose area the child is ordinarily resident; and
(c) that local authority are of the opinion that it would be in the child’s best interests for the applicant’s responsibilities under the order to be transferred to them,
SUBJECT TO their having complied with any requirements imposed by the regulations.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR Regulations made under subsection (3)
TO, in particular, make provision as to—
(a) the considerations to which the local authority shall have regard in forming an opinion as mentioned in subsection (3)(c); and
(b) the time at which responsibility under any emergency protection order is to be treated as having been transferred to a local authority.
