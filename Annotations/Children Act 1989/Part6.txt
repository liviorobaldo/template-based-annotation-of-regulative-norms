------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR every local authority
TO make such arrangements as they consider appropriate for securing that homes (“community homes”) are available—
(a) for the care and accommodation of children looked after by them; and
(b) for purposes connected with the welfare of children (whether or not looked after by them),
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR every local authority
TO do so jointly with one or more other local authorities.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR a local authority
TO have regard to the need for ensuring the availability of accommodation—
(a) of different descriptions; and
(b) which is suitable for different purposes and the requirements of different descriptions of children
WHEN/IF/WHERE making such arrangements.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a local authority
TO make arrangements for the management by another person of accommodation provided by the local authority for the purpose of restricting the liberty of children.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a local authority
TO, with the consent of the body of managers constituted by the instrument of management for the home, make arrangements for the management by another person of accommodation provided for the purpose of restricting the liberty of children
WHEN/IF/WHERE the local authority are to be responsible for the management of a community home provided by a voluntary organisation.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR a local authority
TO designate the home as a controlled community home
WHEN/IF/WHERE the local authority are to be responsible for the management of a community home provided by a voluntary organisation.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR a local authority
TO designate the home as an assisted community home
WHEN/IF/WHERE a voluntary organisation are to be responsible for the management of a community home provided by the organisation.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR any dispute relating to a controlled community home
TO be referred by either party to the Secretary of State for his determination
WHEN/IF/WHERE the dispute arises between the local authority specified in the home’s instrument of management and—
(a) the voluntary organisation by which the home is provided; or
(b) any other local authority who have placed, or desire or are required to place, in the home a child who is looked after by them,
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR any dispute relating to an assisted community home
TO be referred by either party to the Secretary of State for his determination
WHEN/IF/WHERE the dispute arises between the voluntary organisation by which the home is provided and any local authority who have placed, or desire to place, in the home a child who is looked after by them.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the Secretary of State
TO, in order to give effect to his determination of a dispute, give such directions as he thinks fit to the local authority or voluntary organisation concerned
WHEN/IF/WHERE the dispute is referred to the Secretary of State under this section.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a dispute which is capable of being dealt with in accordance with a provision whereby a bishop or any other ecclesiastical or denominational authority has power to decide questions relating to religious instruction given in the home
TO be referred to the Secretary of State under this section
WHEN/IF/WHERE any trust deed relating to a controlled or assisted community home contains that provision.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR The voluntary organisation by which a controlled or assisted community home is provided
TO cease to provide the home 
UNLESS they have given to the Secretary of State and to the local authority specified in the home’s instrument of management not less than two years’ notice in writing of their intention to do so.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR a notice under subsection (1)
TO specify the date from which the voluntary organisation intend to cease to provide the home as a community home.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the Secretary of State
TO, by order—
(a) revoke the home’s instrument of management; and
(b) require the local authority who were specified in that instrument to conduct the home until—
(i) the date specified in the subsection (1) notice; or
(ii) such earlier date (if any) as may be specified for the purposes of this paragraph in the order,
as if it were a community home provided by the local authority
WHEN/IF/WHERE a notice is given under subsection (1) and the home’s managers give notice in writing to the Secretary of State that they are unable or unwilling to continue as its managers until the date specified in the subsection (1) notice.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR something in the trust deed for the home
TO affect the conduct of the home by the local authority
WHEN/IF/WHERE the Secretary of State imposes a requirement under subsection (4)(b).
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the Secretary of State
TO by order direct that for the purposes of any provision specified in the direction and made by or under any enactment relating to community homes (other than this section) the home shall, until the date or earlier date specified as mentioned in subsection (4)(b), be treated as a controlled or assisted community home
WHEN/IF/WHERE the Secretary of State imposes a requirement under subsection (4)(b).
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the home
TO until that date be treated for the purposes of any such enactment as a community home provided by the local authority
WHEN/IF/WHERE the Secretary of State imposes a requirement under subsection (4)(b) 
UNLESS the Secretary of State directs otherwise.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The local authority specified in the instrument of management for a controlled or assisted community home
TO give—
(a) the Secretary of State; and
(b) the voluntary organisation by which the home is provided,
not less than two years’ notice in writing of their intention to withdraw their designation of the home as a controlled or assisted community home.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR A notice under subsection (1)
TO specify the date (“the specified date”) on which the designation is to be withdrawn.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the Secretary of State
TO by order revoke the home’s instrument of management from such date earlier than the specified date as may be specified in the order
WHEN/IF/WHERE —
(a) a notice is given under subsection (1) in respect of a controlled or assisted community home;
(b) the home’s managers give notice in writing to the Secretary of State that they are unable or unwilling to continue as managers until the specified date; and
(c) the managers’ notice is not withdrawn.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the Secretary of State
TO consult the local authority and voluntary organisation 
BEFORE making an order under subsection (3).
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR The appropriate compensation
TO be paid—
(a) in the case of compensation payable under subsection (2), to the authority who were the responsible authority at the relevant time; and
(b) in any other case, to the Secretary of State.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR The amount of any sum payable under this section
TO be determined in accordance with such arrangements—
(a) as may be agreed between the voluntary organisation by which the home is, at the relevant time, provided and the responsible authority or (as the case may be) the Secretary of State; or
(b) in default of agreement, as may be determined by the Secretary of State.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the liability to pay any sum under this section
TO, With the agreement of the responsible authority or (as the case may be) the Secretary of State, be discharged, in whole or in part, by the transfer of any premises.
