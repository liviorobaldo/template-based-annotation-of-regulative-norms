------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the child’s welfare
TO be the court’s paramount consideration
WHEN/IF/WHERE a court determines any question with respect to—
(a) the upbringing of a child; or
(b) the administration of a child’s property or the application of any income arising from it
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have regard to the general principle that any delay in determining the question is likely to prejudice the welfare of the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE any question with respect to the upbringing of a child arises in any proceedings.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have to regard the welfare checklist factors in paragraphs (a) to (g) of section 1(3)
WHEN/IF/WHERE the court is considering whether to make, vary, or discharge a section 8 order and that action is opposed by any party to the proceedings
UNLESS the court is not taking into consideration whether to vary, make, or discharge a special guardianship order or an order under Part IV of the Act.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR a court
TO have regard in particular to—
(a) the ascertainable wishes and feelings of the child concerned (considered in the light of his age and understanding);
(b) his physical, emotional and educational needs;
(c) the likely effect on him of any change in his circumstances;
(d) his age, sex, background and any characteristics of his which the court considers relevant;
(e) any harm which he has suffered or is at risk of suffering;
(f) how capable each of his parents, and any other person in relation to whom the court considers the question to be relevant, is of meeting his needs;
(g) the range of powers available to the court under this Act in the proceedings in question
WHEN/IF/WHERE the circumstances mentioned in subsection (4) occur.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a court
TO make the order or any of the orders
WHEN/IF/WHERE the court is considering whether or not to make one or more orders under this Act with respect to a child
UNLESS it considers that doing so would be better for the child than making no order at all.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the child's father and mother
TO each have parental responsibility for the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE they were married to, or civil partners of, each other at the time of the child's birth.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the child's mother
TO have parental responsibility for the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child’s father and mother were not married to, or civil partners of, each other at the time of his birth
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the child's father
TO have parental responsibility for the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE he has acquired it (and has not ceased to have it) in accordance with the provisions of this Act.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the child's mother
TO have parental responsibility for the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE a child has a parent by virtue of section 43 of the Human Fertilisation and Embryology Act 2008 and is not a person to whom section 1(3) of the Family Law Reform Act 1987 applies
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the other parent of the child
TO have parental responsibility for the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE a child has a parent by virtue of section 43 of the Human Fertilisation and Embryology Act 2008 and is not a person to whom section 1(3) of the Family Law Reform Act 1987 applies
WHEN/IF/WHERE the mother has acquired parental responsibility for the child (and has not ceased to have it) in accordance with the provisions of this Act.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR more than one person
TO have parental responsibility for the same child simultaneously
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a person with parental responsibility for a child
TO act alone and without the other (or others) in meeting that responsibility
WHEN/IF/WHERE  more than one person has parental responsibility for a child
SUBJECT TO any enactment which requires the consent of more than one person in a matter affecting the child.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a person who has parental responsibility for a child
TO act in any way which would be incompatible with any order made with respect to the child under this Act.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a person who has parental responsibility for a child
TO surrender or transfer any part of that responsibility to another
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR A person who has parental responsibility for a child
TO arrange for some or all of that responsibility to be met by one or more persons acting on his behalf.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The person with whom any such arrangement is made
TO himself be a person who already has parental responsibility for the child concerned.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR A person who—
(a) does not have parental responsibility for a particular child; but
(b) has care of the child,
TO do what is reasonable in all the circumstances of the case for the purpose of safeguarding or promoting the child’s welfare
SUBJECT TO the provisions of this Act.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the father of a child
TO acquire parental responsibility
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child’s father and mother were not married to, or civil partners of, each other at the time of his birth
WHEN/IF/WHERE (a) he becomes registered as the child’s father under any of the enactments specified in subsection (1A);
(b) he and the child’s mother make an agreement (a “parental responsibility agreement”) providing for him to have parental responsibility for the child; or
(c) the court, on his application, orders that he shall have parental responsibility for the child.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The Secretary of State
TO, by order, amend subsection (1A) so as to add further enactments to the list in that subsection.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a parental responsibility agreement
TO have effect for the purposes of this Act
UNLESS (a) it is made in the form prescribed by regulations made by the Lord Chancellor; and
(b) where regulations are made by the Lord Chancellor prescribing the manner in which such agreements must be recorded, it is recorded in the prescribed manner.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The court
TO make an order under subsection (2A) on the application—
(a) of any person who has parental responsibility for the child; or
(b) with the leave of the court, of the child himself,
SUBJECT TO, in the case of parental responsibility acquired under subsection (1)(c), section 12(4).
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The court
TO only grant leave under subsection (3)(b)
WHEN/IF/WHERE it is satisfied that the child has sufficient understanding to make the proposed application.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a child’s parent (“parent A”) or, if the other parent of the child also has parental responsibility for the child, both parents
TO by agreement with a person who is not the child’s parent (“the step-parent”) provide for the step-parent to have parental responsibility for the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child’s parent who has parental responsibility for the child is married to, or a civil partner of, the step-parent
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO, on the application of a person who is not the child’s parent (“the step-parent”), order that the step-parent shall have parental responsibility for the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child’s parent who has parental responsibility for the child is married to, or a civil partner of, the step-parent.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The court
TO only grant leave under subsection (3)(b)
WHEN/IF/WHERE it is satisfied that the child has sufficient understanding to make the proposed application.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO, by order, appoint that individual to be the child’s guardian if—
(a) the child has no parent with parental responsibility for him; or
(b) a parent, guardian or special guardian of the child's was named in a child arrangements order as a person with whom the child was to live and has died while the order was in force ; or
(c) paragraph (b) does not apply, and the child’s only or last surviving special guardian dies
WHEN/IF/WHERE an application with respect to a child is made to the court by any individual
UNLESS the child arrangements order referred to in paragraph (b) also named a surviving parent of the child as a person with whom the child was to live.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The power conferred by subsection (1)
TO also be exercised in any family proceedings
WHEN/IF/WHERE the court considers that the order should be made even though no application has been made for it.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR A parent who has parental responsibility for his child
TO appoint another individual to be the child’s guardian in the event of their death.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR A guardian of a child
TO appoint another individual to take his place as the child’s guardian in the event of his death
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a special guardian of a child
TO appoint another individual to be the child’s guardian in the event of his death.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR an appointment under section 5(3) (by either a parent or guardian) or section 5(4) (by a guardian or special guardian)
TO have effect
UNLESS it is made in writing, is dated and is signed by the person making the appointment or—
(a) in the case of an appointment made by a will which is not signed by the testator, is signed at the direction of the testator in accordance with the requirements of section 9 of the Wills Act 1837; or
(b) in any other case, is signed at the direction of the person making the appointment, in his presence and in the presence of two witnesses who each attest the signature.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR A person appointed as a child’s guardian under this section
TO have parental responsibility for the child concerned.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a court
TO exercise the High Court’s inherent jurisdiction to appoint a guardian of the estate of any child
SUBJECT TO any provision made by rules of court
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR rules of court
TO prescribe the circumstances in which, and conditions subject to which, an appointment of such a guardian may be made
WHEN/IF/WHERE rules of court are made under subsection (11)
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR A guardian of a child
TO only be appointed in accordance with the provisions of this section
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the person who made an appointment under section 5(3) or (4) (including one made in an unrevoked will or codicil)
TO revoke it by a written and dated instrument which is signed—
(a) by him; or
(b) at his direction, in his presence and in the presence of two witnesses who each attest the signature.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the person who made an appointment under section 5(3) or (4) (including one made in an unrevoked will or codicil)
TO, with the intention of revoking the appointment, 
(a) destroy the instrument by which it was made; or
(b) have some other person destroy that instrument in his presence.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR A person who is appointed as a guardian under section 5(3) or (4)
TO disclaim his appointment by an instrument in writing signed by him and made within a reasonable time of his first knowing that the appointment has taken effect.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR Any appointment of a guardian under section 5
TO be brought to an end at any time by order of the court—
(a) on the application of any person who has parental responsibility for the child;
(b) on the application of the child concerned, with leave of the court; or
(c) in any family proceedings, if the court considers that it should be brought to an end even though no application has been made.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR A court considering any question with respect to a child under this Act
TO (a) ask an officer of the Service or a Welsh family proceedings officer ; or
(b) ask a local authority to arrange for—
(i) an officer of the authority; or
(ii) such other person (other than an officer of the Service or a Welsh family proceedings officer ) as the authority considers appropriate,
to report to the court on such matters relating to the welfare of that child as are required to be dealt with in the report.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The Lord Chancellor
TO make regulations specifying matters which, unless the court orders otherwise, must be dealt with in any report under this section
AFTER consulting the Lord Chief Justice
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The report
TO be made in writing, or orally, as the court requires.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR a court
TO take account of—
(a) any statement contained in the report; and
(b) any evidence given in respect of the matters referred to in the report,
in so far as the statement or evidence is, in the opinion of the court, relevant to the question which it is considering, regardless of any enactment or rule of law which would otherwise prevent it from doing so.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR The Lord Chief Justice
TO nominate a judicial office holder (as defined in section 109(4) of the Constitutional Reform Act 2005) to exercise his functions under subsection (2).
