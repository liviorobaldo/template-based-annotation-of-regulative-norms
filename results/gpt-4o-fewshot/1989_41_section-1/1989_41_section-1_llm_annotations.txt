IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO consider the child’s welfare as the paramount consideration
WHEN/IF/WHERE a court determines any question with respect to— (a) the upbringing of a child; or (b) the administration of a child’s property or the application of any income arising from it [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have regard to the general principle that any delay in determining the question is likely to prejudice the welfare of the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE any proceedings in which any question with respect to the upbringing of a child arises [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO presume that involvement of each parent will further the child's welfare
WHEN/IF/WHERE in the circumstances mentioned in subsection (4)(a) or (7) [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
UNLESS the contrary is shown [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have regard in particular to the factors listed in subsection (3)
WHEN/IF/WHERE in the circumstances mentioned in subsection (4) [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR the court
TO make the order or any of the orders
WHEN/IF/WHERE the court is considering whether or not to make one or more orders under this Act with respect to a child [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
UNLESS it considers that doing so would be better for the child than making no order at all [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
