IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO make a section 8 order with respect to the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE in any family proceedings in which a question arises with respect to the welfare of any child [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
SUBJECT TO the restrictions imposed by section 9 [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a person
TO apply for a section 8 order with respect to any child
WHEN/IF/WHERE the person is entitled to apply for a section 8 order with respect to the child or has obtained the leave of the court to make the application [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a person
TO apply for a child arrangements order
WHEN/IF/WHERE the person is a local authority foster parent and the child has lived with him for a period of at least one year immediately preceding the application [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a relative of a child
TO apply for a child arrangements order
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child has lived with the relative for a period of at least one year immediately preceding the application [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have particular regard to certain factors
WHEN/IF/WHERE deciding whether or not to grant leave for a person who is not the child to make an application for a section 8 order [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO be satisfied that the child has sufficient understanding to make the proposed application
WHEN/IF/WHERE the person applying for leave to make an application for a section 8 order is the child concerned [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
