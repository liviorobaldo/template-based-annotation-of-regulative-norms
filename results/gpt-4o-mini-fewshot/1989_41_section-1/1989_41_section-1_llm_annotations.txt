IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO make the child’s welfare the paramount consideration
WHEN a court determines any question with respect to— (a) the upbringing of a child; or (b) the administration of a child’s property or the application of any income arising from it [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have regard to the general principle that any delay in determining the question is likely to prejudice the welfare of the child
WHEN in any proceedings in which any question with respect to the upbringing of a child arises [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO presume that involvement of that parent in the life of the child concerned will further the child's welfare
UNLESS the contrary is shown [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have regard in particular to the ascertainable wishes and feelings of the child concerned
WHEN in the circumstances mentioned in subsection (4) [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO not make the order or any of the orders
WHEN the court is considering whether or not to make one or more orders under this Act with respect to a child [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
UNLESS it considers that doing so would be better for the child than making no order at all [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have regard to the physical, emotional and educational needs of the child
WHEN in the circumstances mentioned in subsection (4) [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
