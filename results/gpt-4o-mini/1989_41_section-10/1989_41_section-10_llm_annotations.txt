FOR the court

IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the court
TO make a section 8 order with respect to any child on the application of a person who—
IF is entitled to apply for a section 8 order with respect to the child; [source: section-10-2-a]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the court
TO make a section 8 order with respect to any child on the application of a person who—
IF has obtained the leave of the court to make the application. [source: section-10-2-b]

FOR the court
TO make section 8 orders
SUBJECT TO the restrictions imposed by section 9. [source: section-10-3]

IT IS PERMITTED
FOR any parent, guardian or special guardian of the child
TO apply to the court for any section 8 order with respect to a child
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR any person who by virtue of section 4A has parental responsibility for the child
TO apply to the court for any section 8 order with respect to a child
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR any person who is named in a child arrangements order that is in force with respect to the child
TO apply to the court for any section 8 order with respect to a child
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR any party to a marriage in relation to whom the child is a child of the family
TO apply for a child arrangements order with respect to a child
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR any civil partner in a civil partnership in relation to whom the child is a child of the family
TO apply for a child arrangements order with respect to a child
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR any person with whom the child has lived for a period of at least three years
TO apply for a child arrangements order with respect to a child
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR any person who has parental responsibility for the child by virtue of provision made under section 12(2A)
TO apply for a child arrangements order with respect to a child
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a local authority foster parent
TO apply for a child arrangements order to which subsection (5C) applies with respect to a child
IF the child has lived with him for a period of at least one year immediately preceding the application.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a relative of a child
TO apply for a child arrangements order to which subsection (5C) applies with respect to the child
IF the child has lived with the relative for a period of at least one year immediately preceding the application.
------------------------

TO apply for the variation or discharge of a section 8 order
IF the order was made on his application; [source: section-10-6-a]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a person who would not otherwise be entitled to apply for the variation or discharge of a section 8 order
TO apply for the variation or discharge of a section 8 order
IF in the case of a child arrangements order, he is named in provisions of the order regulating arrangements relating to— [source: section-10-6-b]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR any person who falls within a category of person prescribed by rules of court
TO apply for any such section 8 order as may be prescribed in relation to that category of person
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR an application for a child arrangements order to which subsection (7B) applies
TO be made with respect to him
ONLY IF apart from this subsection the leave of the court is not required, with such leave.

IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO grant leave
ONLY IF it is satisfied that he has sufficient understanding to make the proposed application for the section 8 order.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have particular regard to—
WHEN the person applying for leave to make an application for a section 8 order is not the child concerned
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have particular regard to the nature of the proposed application for the section 8 order
WHEN the person applying for leave to make an application for a section 8 order is not the child concerned
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the applicant
TO ensure that the period of three years mentioned in subsection (5)(b) need not be continuous but must not have begun more than five years before, or ended more than three months before, the making of the application.
