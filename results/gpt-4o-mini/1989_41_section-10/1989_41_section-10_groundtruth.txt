IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO in any family proceedings in which a question arises with respect to the welfare of any child, make a section 8 order with respect to the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE (a) an application for the order has been made by a person who—(i) is entitled to apply for a section 8 order with respect to the child; or (ii) has obtained the leave of the court to make the application; or (b) the court considers that the order should be made even though no such application has been made [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
SUBJECT TO the restrictions in section 9. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO make a section 8 order with respect to any child on the application of a person who— (a) is entitled to apply for a section 8 order with respect to the child; or (b) has obtained the leave of the court to make the application
SUBJECT TO the restrictions in section 9. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR (a) any party to a marriage (whether or not subsisting) in relation to whom the child is a child of the family;
TO to apply for a child arrangements order with respect to the child.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a local authority foster parent
TO apply for a child arrangements order relating only to with whom the child is to live and when the child is to live with any person
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child has lived with the foster parent for at least one year immediately preceding the application. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a relative of a child
TO apply for a child arrangements order relating only to with whom the child is to live and when the child is to live with any person
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child has lived with the relative for at least one year immediately preceding the application. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR A person who would not otherwise be entitled (under the previous provisions of this section) to apply for the variation or discharge of a section 8 order
TO apply for its variation or discharge
WHEN/IF/WHERE (a) the order was made on his application; or (b) in the case of a child arrangements order, he is named in provisions of the order regulating arrangements relating to— (i) with whom the child concerned is to spend time or otherwise have contact, or (ii) when the child is to spend time or otherwise have contact with any person. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR Any person who falls within a category of person prescribed by rules of court
TO apply for any such section 8 order as may be prescribed in relation to that category of person.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR an application for a child arrangements order to which subsection (7B) applies
TO be made with respect to him
ONLY IF apart from this subsection the leave of the court is not required, with such leave [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
WHEN/IF/WHERE a special guardianship order is in force with respect to the child. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR the court
TO grant leave
ONLY IF it is satisfied that he has sufficient understanding to make the proposed application for the section 8 order [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
WHEN/IF/WHERE the person applying for leave to make an application for a section 8 order is the child concerned [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have particular regard to— (a) the nature of the proposed application for the section 8 order; (b) the applicant’s connection with the child; (c) any risk there might be of that proposed application disrupting the child’s life to such an extent that he would be harmed by it; and (d) where the child is being looked after by a local authority— (i) the authority’s plans for the child’s future; and (ii) the wishes and feelings of the child’s parents
WHEN/IF/WHERE the person applying for leave to make an application for a section 8 order is not the child concerned [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]
WHEN/IF/WHERE deciding whether or not to grant leave. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/10]

TO have begun more than five years before, or ended more than three months before, the making of the application.
