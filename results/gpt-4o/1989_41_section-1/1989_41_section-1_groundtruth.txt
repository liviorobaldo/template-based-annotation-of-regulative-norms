IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the child’s welfare
TO be the court’s paramount consideration
WHEN/IF/WHERE a court determines any question with respect to— (a) the upbringing of a child; or (b) the administration of a child’s property or the application of any income arising from it [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have regard to the general principle that any delay in determining the question is likely to prejudice the welfare of the child
WHEN/IF/WHERE any question with respect to the upbringing of a child arises in any proceedings. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the court
TO have to regard the welfare checklist factors in paragraphs (a) to (g) of section 1(3)
WHEN/IF/WHERE the court is considering whether to make, vary, or discharge a section 8 order and that action is opposed by any party to the proceedings [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
UNLESS the court is not taking into consideration whether to vary, make, or discharge a special guardianship order or an order under Part IV of the Act. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR a court
TO have regard in particular to— (a) the ascertainable wishes and feelings of the child concerned (considered in the light of his age and understanding); (b) his physical, emotional and educational needs; (c) the likely effect on him of any change in his circumstances; (d) his age, sex, background and any characteristics of his which the court considers relevant; (e) any harm which he has suffered or is at risk of suffering; (f) how capable each of his parents, and any other person in relation to whom the court considers the question to be relevant, is of meeting his needs; (g) the range of powers available to the court under this Act in the proceedings in question
WHEN/IF/WHERE the circumstances mentioned in subsection (4) occur. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a court
TO make the order or any of the orders
WHEN/IF/WHERE the court is considering whether or not to make one or more orders under this Act with respect to a child [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
UNLESS it considers that doing so would be better for the child than making no order at all. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/1]
