IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO ask an officer of the Service or a Welsh family proceedings officer— (a) to monitor, or arrange for the monitoring of, the individual's compliance with the direction (or the condition); (b) to report to the court on any failure by the individual to comply with the direction (or the condition)
WHEN/IF/WHERE making the direction (or imposing the condition by means of a child arrangements order). [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/11G]
WHEN/IF/WHERE in any family proceedings the court— (a) makes an activity direction in relation to an individual, or (b) makes a child arrangements order that imposes, or varies a child arrangements order so as to impose, an activity condition on an individual. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/11G]

IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the officer of the Service or Welsh family proceedings officer
TO any request under subsection (2)
WHEN/IF/WHERE in any family proceedings the court— (a) makes an activity direction in relation to an individual, or (b) makes a child arrangements order that imposes, or varies a child arrangements order so as to impose, an activity condition on an individual. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/11G]

IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO ask an officer of the Service or a Welsh family proceedings officer— (a) to monitor whether an individual falling within subsection (3) complies with each provision of any of those kinds that is contained in the child arrangements order (or in the child arrangements order as varied); (b) to report to the court on such matters relating to the individual's compliance as the court may specify in the request.
WHEN/IF/WHERE in any family proceedings the court makes— (a) a child arrangements order containing provision of a kind mentioned in section 11C(1)(a)(i), (ii) or (iii), or (b) an order varying a child arrangements order so as to add, vary or omit provision of any of those kinds. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/11G]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO make a request under subsection (2)— (a) on making the child arrangements order(or the order varying the child arrangements order), or (b) at any time during the subsequent course of the proceedings as they relate to contact with the child concerned or to the child's living arrangements.

WHEN/IF/WHERE making a request under subsection (2). [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/11G]
