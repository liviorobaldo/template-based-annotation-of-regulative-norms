IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a court
TO make an activity direction in connection with that provision of the child arrangements order.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR a court
TO on the same occasion, — (a) make an activity direction under subsection (2B), and (b) dispose finally of the proceedings as they relate to failure to comply with the provision in connection with which the activity direction is made.
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR A court
TO make an activity direction under section 11A(2) in connection with any matter mentioned in section 11A(1A)
UNLESS there is a dispute as regards the provision about that matter that the court is considering whether to make in the proceedings. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/11A]
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR A court
TO make an activity direction requiring an individual who is a child to take part in an activity
UNLESS the individual is a parent of the child in relation to whom the court is considering provision about a matter mentioned in section 11A(1A). [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/11A]
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR A court
TO make an activity activity direction in connection with the making, variation or discharge of a child arrangements order
WHEN/IF/WHERE the child arrangements order is, or would if made be, an excepted order. [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/11A]
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR A court
TO make an activity direction in relation to an individual
UNLESS the individual is habitually resident in England and Wales [source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/41/section/11A]
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a child arrangements order
TO impose a condition (an "activity condition") requiring an individual falling within subsection (3) to take part in an activity that would, in the court's opinion, help to establish, maintain or improve the involvement in the life of the child concerned of— (a) that individual, or (b) another individual who is a party to the proceedings.
------------------------
IT IS PERMITTED
FOR a child arrangements order
TO be varied so as to impose a condition (an "activity condition") requiring an individual falling within subsection (3) to take part in an activity that would, in the court's opinion, help to establish, maintain or improve the involvement in the life of the child concerned of— (a) that individual, or (b) another individual who is a party to the proceedings.
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR the condition
TO specify the activity and the person providing the activity.
