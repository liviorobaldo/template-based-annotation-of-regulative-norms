IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR court
TO consider the child’s welfare as the paramount consideration
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR court
TO have regard to the general principle that any delay in determining the question is likely to prejudice the welfare of the child
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR court
TO presume that involvement of a parent will further the child's welfare unless the contrary is shown
SUBJECT TO the circumstances mentioned in subsection (4)(a) or (7) [source: section-1-2A]
------------------------
IT IS PROHIBITED
FOR court
TO make an order unless it considers that doing so would be better for the child than making no order at all
WHEN considering whether or not to make one or more orders under this Act with respect to a child [source: section-1-5]
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR parent
TO be involved in the child's life in a way that does not put the child at risk of suffering harm
------------------------
IT IS OBLIGATORY
FOR court
TO treat a parent as being within paragraph (a) unless there is evidence suggesting that involvement would put the child at risk of suffering harm
